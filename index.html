High-Level Comparative Takeaways

CPU economics dominate or disappear

With Xeon 8468H the CPU is ~80% of the spend; with the 8558 it falls below 25%. Intel’s own list prices are ~$13.9k for 8468H and ~$4.65k for 8558; the Dell-SG quote is an extreme regional markup.

GPU spend is steady

Both Ada-generation cards carry identical 48 GB VRAM. The PNY-branded board shaves ≈ $1k without hurting compute performance; the NVIDIA-branded card may add better blower acoustics and 3-year direct support.

Memory & storage are rounding errors at this tier

Jumping between the two 256 GB ECC kits changes the grand total by < 5%. If you need >256 GB, budget rises linearly (~$750 per extra 128 GB).

Motherboard choice is about compatibility, not cost

The ASUS W790 board is aimed at single-socket Xeon W; verify VRM support before ordering. The Supermicro X13DAI-T-O is validated for 4th & 5th-gen Xeon Scalable and is the pragmatic pick.

Performance Nuance

Both CPUs share the same core/thread counts, but the 8558 doubles L3 cache (260MB vs 105MB) and has faster UPI (20 GT/s vs 16 GT/s). In memory-bound HPC workloads the 8558 may outperform despite the lower vendor price.
